Silage is a feed for animals that's made by fermenting green plants in the absence of oxygen. The process of making silage, also known as ensilage, preserves nutrients in the plants and allows them to be fed to animals all year.

Here are some things to know about silage:

What crops can be used

Silage can be made from many different types of green plants, including corn, legumes, grasses, and weeds.

How it's made

The process of making silage involves cutting and chopping the plants, then allowing them to wilt in the field to reduce moisture content. The plants are then placed in a silo and left to ferment.

Why it's made

Silage is made to preserve nutrients and provide a good quality food for animals, especially during lean seasons when there's less green fodder available.

How it's stored

Silage is often formed into cylindrical bales and sealed in polywrap.

How it's eaten

Silage is eaten by many different types of animals, including cattle, sheep, donkeys, camels, chickens, rabbits, geese, goats, and pigs.
