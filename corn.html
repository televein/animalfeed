Product Details - Maize Silage

Process of Making Maize Silage

The process of making maize silage involves cutting, chopping, and fermenting the green maize plants to preserve their nutrients for livestock feeding.

Once the plants are chopped, they are compacted to remove oxygen and sealed tightly to allow fermentation. This process produces high-quality feed for cattle year-round.

Ingredients in Maize Silage

  • Whole maize plant

  • Fibers (important for digestion)

  • Carbohydrates (energy source for cattle)

  • Proteins (for growth and muscle development)

Nutrients in Maize Silage

  • High energy content from carbohydrates

  • Fiber for improved digestion

  • Protein for muscle and tissue growth

  • Vitamins A and E for immune system support

Benefits of Maize Silage for Cattle

  • Improved digestion and gut health

  • Steady supply of energy, especially during dry seasons

  • Promotes weight gain and milk production

  • Enhances overall health and productivity of cattle
